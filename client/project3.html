Magic Toy Shop

The Magic Toy Shop is a retailer of toys, games, and magic tricks. For over 30 years it has successfully focused on traditional toys, but now offers a full range of new and vintage products to appeal to children and adults alike. The shop specializes in sourcing the most cherished items from everyoneâ€™s childhood, making long-forgotten things available today.Through their new website, it wanted to showcase its products, while maintaining the brand image of tradition, fun, and creativity.



Duration: 2 weeks

Team: Me



Tools & Methods

Information architecture, open and closed card sorting, user research, competitive and comparative analysis, site map, user flows, wire flows, prototyping, interaction design, sketching, personas

The Problem

Maintaining tradition while keeping up with new trends.

The Magic Toy Shop had a problem with attracting both children and adults alike while maintaining the sentiment of a highly curated brick and mortar shop. Competing with giant retailers such as Amazon, the Magic Toy Shop was facing possible extinction if the brand image didn't keep up. This is where I needed to step in.

The Solution

Plan for scalability while maintaining a small shop feel.

By incorporating content strategy management to tackle new inventory, adding a "chat with a rep" feature, and implementing a reviews database, the new and improved Magic Toy shop would be able to cater to a wide variety of users.

Whether a parent is searching for a gift for their child, or a vintage collector is seeking the new collectable, the Magic Toy Shop will contain an intuitively organized database of toys along with several features that will ensure the company remains competitive and relevant.

The Process

Apply information architecture techniques for a seamless e-commerce experience.

Research

In order to be able to establish a streamlined experience for e-commerce users a lot of research had to be done. I began by synthesizing data into primary personas, who I referred back to throughout the process to make sure design decisions were data-driven. Once the personas were establiched I recruited people for an open card sorting activity that aimed at uncovering what an intuitive way of categorizing toys and games would be. I was able to extract common categories from this activity, and proceeded to conduct a closed card sorting with some categories already established in order to validate them with users. With the information architecture determined, I moved forward with the process.



Implementation

Consequently, I drafted a sitemap for the new Magic Toy Shop to get an idea of what the skeleton would look like. Then, I created user flows for the personas so I could visualize what key touchpoints the users would have with the system. This was an integral part of my process that steered the ideation of wire flows and user interface sketching . The final product was an effective e-commerce website that served a multitude of user needs.
